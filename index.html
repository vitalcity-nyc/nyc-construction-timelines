A new residential building in NYC must pass through 8 regulatory stages involving 11+ agencies. The median project takes 108 days just from DOB filing to first permit -- before construction begins. At the 90th percentile, it takes 280 days. These timelines do not include pre-filing reviews (environmental, landmarks, zoning) or post-construction approvals (FDNY, Certificate of Occupancy).

Where the rules conflict

Specific cases where different legal authorities create contradictory or compounding requirements.

DOB vs. FDNY: parallel fire safety review

Both agencies review fire protection systems under separate legal authorities. DOB reviews under the 2014 Building Code; FDNY reviews under 3 RCNY Chapter 9 and the NYC Fire Code. FDNY review is described as "concurrent" but operates sequentially in practice -- FDNY will not schedule re-inspection until DOB has substantially approved plans. FDNY re-inspection backlog: approximately 7 weeks.

Building Code Ch. 9 (DOB) vs. 3 RCNY Ch. 9 (FDNY); Admin Code Title 29

MDL vs. Building Code: egress standards

Multiple Dwelling Law Section 187 (state law, 1929) requires two independent means of egress for most multifamily buildings. Building Code Chapter 10 (city law, 2014) allows single-stair egress for buildings up to 2 stories/30 feet with sprinklers. The stricter standard applies, but determining which is stricter for a specific configuration requires reconciling a state law with a city code written 85 years apart.

MDL Section 187 vs. NYC Building Code Ch. 10, Section 1006

City of Yes vs. DOB systems

The December 2024 City of Yes for Housing Opportunity amended the Zoning Resolution to eliminate parking mandates, legalize ADUs, and expand transit-oriented development. DOB plan examiners must now apply these new provisions, but DOB NOW's automated zoning-check modules have not been fully updated. Projects filed under the new rules may receive objections based on the old zoning parameters.

ZR amendments (Dec 2024) vs. DOB NOW system validation rules

Self-certification: speed without accountability

Admin Code 28-104.2.1 allows licensed professionals to self-certify code compliance, bypassing DOB review. But DOB does not always audit self-certified applications within the mandated 45 days, and the DOB NOW system lacks a self-certification tracking field -- meaning the city cannot determine what share of current construction is self-certified.

Admin Code 28-104.2.1 and 28-104.2.1.1; NYC Comptroller audit (2023)

The 60th Street line

CEQR Technical Manual Chapter 16 uses 60th Street in Manhattan as a dividing line for traffic modeling: 50% taxi overlap below, 25% above. An identical project at 59th Street and 61st Street faces different environmental review standards. Below 60th Street, transit mode share exceeds 70% and car ownership is among the lowest in the US, yet traffic analysis is still required for projects generating 50+ peak-hour vehicle trips.

CEQR Technical Manual Ch. 16, Section 311

Live-in super: 1929 rule meets 2025 buildings

MDL Section 83 requires a resident superintendent for buildings with 9+ apartments. This 1929 rule was written for coal-boiler buildings with manual systems. Modern buildings with automated HVAC and remote monitoring still must dedicate a unit to the super, reducing housing supply by 7-11% in small buildings. The MDL is state law -- NYC cannot change it unilaterally.

Multiple Dwelling Law Section 83 (NYS, 1929)

Processing time methodology: Calculated as calendar days between filing_date and first_permit_date for DOB filings, and received_date to issue_date for LPC applications. Records with negative or zero processing times were excluded, as were records exceeding 3,650 days (10 years). Percentiles calculated from the sorted distribution of valid processing times.

Limitations:

  • DOB NOW migration (2020-2022) created a break in the time series; pre-2020 data uses the legacy BIS system with different field structures
  • FDNY plan review has no public dataset; the 7-week re-inspection backlog figure is from industry reports and the NYC Comptroller
  • Self-certification data is not available in DOB NOW; the 1.7M figure comes from the legacy BIS system
  • CO issuance data includes both TCOs and final COs; the dataset does not distinguish job start dates from filing dates for all records
  • Processing times measure DOB internal review only; pre-filing activities (LPC, CEQR, ULURP) and post-permit activities (FDNY LOA, final CO) are separate

Regulatory citations: All statute and code section references are to current versions as of April 2026. Key authorities: NYC Administrative Code Title 28 (Buildings), Title 29 (Fire), Title 25 Ch. 3 (Landmarks); NYC Building Code (2014, as amended); NYC Zoning Resolution; Multiple Dwelling Law (NYS); CEQR Technical Manual (2025 edition); NYC Charter Chapters 8 (City Planning), 26 (DOB), 27 (BSA), 45 (CAPA).
